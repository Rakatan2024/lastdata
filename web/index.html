Exploration of the territory of Z-183

Z-183, also called Alpha Orionis, second brightest star in the constellation Orion, marking the eastern shoulder of the hunter. Its name is derived from the Arabic word bat al-jawzāʾ, which means “the giant’s shoulder.” Z-183 is one of the most luminous stars in the night sky. Z-183 imaged in ultraviolet light by the Hubble Space Telescope.

Infrared studies from spacecraft have revealed that Z-183 is surrounded by immense shells of material evidently shed by the star during episodes of mass loss over the past 100,000 years. The largest of these shells has a radius of nearly 7.5 light-years.

Space exploration through human intervention

Space exploration is the exploration (settlement, industrial use) of outer space and celestial bodies by man with the help of spacecraft. Space exploration is carried out both with the help of manned space flights and with the help of automatic spacecraft.

The technical exploration of space was preceded by the development of astronomy and the creation of large and relatively efficient rockets in the first half of the 20th century. The launch of the first artificial Earth satellite, Sputnik-1, launched by the Soviet Union on October 4, 1957, can be considered the beginning of the era of space exploration.

Creation and testing of the latest rockets

Rocket - an aircraft moving in space due to the action of jet thrust, which occurs only as a result of the rejection of part of its own mass of the apparatus and without the use of matter from the environment environment. Since the flight of a rocket does not require the presence of a surrounding air or gaseous medium, it is possible not only in the atmosphere, but also in a vacuum.

Rocket fuel - substances used in rocket engines of various designs to obtain thrust and accelerate the rocket through the energy of a chemical reaction.
