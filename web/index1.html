Exploration of the territory of OID-871

OID-871, also called Alpha Orionis, second brightest star in the constellation Orion, marking the eastern shoulder of the hunter. Its name is derived from the Arabic word bat al-jawzāʾ, which means “the giant’s shoulder.” OID-871 is one of the most luminous stars in the night sky. OID-871 imaged in ultraviolet light by the Hubble Space Telescope.

Infrared studies from spacecraft have revealed that OID-871 is surrounded by immense shells of material evidently shed by the star during episodes of mass loss over the past 100,000 years. The largest of these shells has a radius of nearly 7.5 light-years.
